   Atomic phobia refers to the deep fear of nuclear weapons and atomic destruction that developed mainly after the invention of the atomic bomb. Books and writings on atomic phobia explain how the devastation of Hiroshima and Nagasaki created long-lasting anxiety in people, leading to constant fear of nuclear war, radiation, and the end of human civilization. 
These works describe the psychological impact of living under the threat of nuclear weapons, showing how this fear influenced human behavior, politics, literature, and culture, especially during the Cold War. 
Overall, such books highlight how atomic phobia shaped modern society by spreading insecurity, stress, and a strong desire for peace and disarmament.
